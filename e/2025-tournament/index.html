Event Details

Half of the registration cost will go towards lunch and snacks.

Registration is limited to the first 16 people. The first 16 people to register and pay their fees will be added to a 16 person bracket.

All games will use the base game only, no expansions. Each bracket match will be played at the 2 player count only. In each match, players will agree to a random method of choosing the first player (coin flip, dice roll, etc.).

Games will be randomly assigned to each player in the bracket. Once advancing, players will never play the same game twice. For the final round, each finalist will be able to veto one game from the remaining games in the pool, and a random game will be selected from the last available games.

Prizes

Each round of the tournament, every winner will recieve a $5 prize. The more games you win, the more money you will recieve.

The grand prize for winning the whole tournament is $10 ($25 total from each preceding round prizes).

The Games

Azul

Weight: 1.77 / 5
Learn to Play Azul Read Azul Rules
  • Players will play the preprinted side of the board.
  • When filling factories, if you draw more than 4 tiles, then all of those tokens will be placed back in the drawbag, with another player having the opportunity to shake the bag before a single replacement tile is drawn.
  • The player without the first player token is responsible for filling factories with new tiles.
  • Scoring should be done one at a time, so the other player may verify you have scored correctly.

Carcassonne

Weight: 1.89 / 5
Learn to Play Carcassonne Read Carcassonne Rules
  • We will be playing with the base Carcassone game, scoring only Knights (cities), Travelers (roads), Monks (monastaries), and Farmers (fields).
  • Cities with two tiles give four points (not two). The Farmer's value is calculated like this: for every meadow the number of farmers is calculated and the player(s) with the most farmers receives 3 points for every city at that meadow.
  • During your opponent's turn you may choose and look at your next tile. The new tile has to be above the tabletop at all times, so it is clear which one you've chosen.
  • In case of an "edge of the table" event you can rearange the tiles in the other direction to get more space. If this is not working we will provide another table to solve the problem.
  • It's not allowed to make any private notes during the game (like counting tiles on a sheet of paper).

Cascadia

Weight: 1.85 / 5
Learn to Play Cascadia Read Cascadia Rules
  • Wildlife tokens must be drawn from the bag one at a time.
  • If more than one is ever drawn at once, then all of those tokens will be placed back in the drawbag, with another player having the opportunity to shake the bag before a single replacement tile is drawn.
