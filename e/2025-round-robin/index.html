Event Details

Half of the registration cost will go towards lunch and snacks.

Registration is limited to the first 16 people. The first 16 people to register and pay their fees will be added to a 16 person round robin tournament.

Except where noted, all games will use the base game only. Every match will be played at the 3 or 4 player count only.

For the initial pool play, games will be randomly assigned to each player in the bracket. Players will never play the same game twice. Each player placing 1st place will receive 3 points. Each player placing 2nd place will receive 2 points. Each player placing 1st place will receive 1 point. The highest 3 scores after 3 games will be placed in a championship match, with one wildcard seat. All players not in the championship match will play one game of Coup, and the winner will win the wildcard seat at the championship game.

The Final, Championship match will be a game of Wingspan.

The details for the tournament (including game selection) are subject to change.

General Rules

Practice good sportsmanship at all times.

When spectating games, you may not make any comments about the gameplay itself that may help either player.

Any individual found to be cheating will be removed from the tournament and will have any winnings won so-far forfeited.

In each match, players will agree to a random method of choosing the first player (coin flip, dice roll, etc.). This will decide the first player, regardless of what the game's rules state.

Players are responsible for helping other players remember to trigger mandatory effects (i.e. Once between turns powers in Wingspan, re-lighting your campfire in PARKS, or remembering to add cards to the clearing when trees are played in Forest Shuffle).

Errors can only be corrected if all players agree, or if a referee decides it can be done.

Players may not resign from games, and must complete games until completion.

In the event where you realize an illegal play has taken place, and no immediate resolution can be agreed upon by both players, players will reset the game and begin from the beginning, with the player who did not make the illegal move deciding first player.

In the event of a rules clarification or dispute, players will call a referee to resolve the dispute. The referee will be clearly declared and agreed upon before gameplay begins.

Pool Play Games

Azul

Weight: 1.77 / 5
Learn to Play Azul Read Azul Rules
  • Players will play the preprinted side of the board.
  • When filling factories, if you draw more than 4 tiles, then all of those tokens will be placed back in the drawbag, with another player having the opportunity to shake the bag before a single replacement tile is drawn.
  • The player with the first player token is responsible for filling factories with new tiles.
  • Scoring should be done one at a time, so the other player may verify you have scored correctly.

Carcassonne

Weight: 1.89 / 5
Learn to Play Carcassonne Read Carcassonne Rules
  • Score only Knights (cities), Travelers (roads), Monks (monastaries), and Farmers (fields)
  • Cities with two tiles give four points (not two).
  • Player(s) with the most farmers receives 3 points for every city in that field.
  • During your opponent's turn you may choose and look at your next tile. The new tile has to be above the tabletop at all times, so it is clear which one you've chosen.
  • In case of an "edge of the table" event, rearange the tiles in the other direction to get more space. If this is not possible, we will shift the game to the floor or a new table.
  • Players are not allowed to take any private notes during the game (like counting tiles on a sheet of paper).

Red Rising

Learn to Play Red Rising Read Red Rising Rules
